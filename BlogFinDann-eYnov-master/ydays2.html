Application and Teamwork !

  • August 16, 2020
  • By Antoine MACHEDA

Among the many IT schools, Campus Ynov has a special feature: the possibility of working on a common project with other students of different level and orientation. As a result, the first years of the computer science bachelor are led to work in cooperation with masters or two and third year not only in computer science, to learn alongside them, but also with students in different sectors such as design, marketing. or even 3D animations. This collaboration around a common project allows for a new experience that is greatly akin to cooperative work as it is practiced in the professional world. During my first year at Ynov Campus, I had the opportunity to work on a campus project, the Student Office Application (BDE).

BDE Application

The basic principle of the BDE application was to allow students on campus to buy or reserve their place for events organized by it. From big theme nights to small bar nights, students could pick up their tickets, pay for them directly in the app with Lydia, and redeem them upon entering the event as a QR code. Lydia is a French application allowing its users to pay, send or receive money between friends or stores supporting Lydia as a payment method. The BDE application has as a connection method the identifiers of Ynov students through a connection portal provided by Microsoft and ensuring its security at the same time. As a result, the students had their own account with the ability to install the application on multiple devices, including Android and IOS, and find their tickets even on their friends' phones. Due to lack of time the application was not completed, but its production has not stopped and its development is still ongoing.

Team and application

Posted by Antoine Macheda

Working in a group on a common project was an opportunity for me to learn and discover areas that were unknown to me, such as design or marketing. The ability to ask questions or be guided by higher level people has been the best learning method and has allowed me to grow exponentially. In addition, good humor and atmosphere being the key value of our group, this work was more like a collaboration between friends, than an academic evaluation.
