A long time ago in a galaxy far, far away...

  • August 16, 2020
  • By Antoine MACHEDA

Since the release of the first computer video game in 1962, millions of games have been released on various platforms. From the traditional computer to the various consoles that exist today, the sale and production of games has experienced a meteoric rise. Whether it is the quality of the graphics on the screens or the virtual reality through the various VR headsets, the evolution of the gaming possibilities is vast. But let’s not forget their origins! These traditional retro games have forged the history of our current games, and they are no less legends. So for my studies, one of my projects was to create a retro-looking video game! From its idealization to its conception, I will reveal the stages of the construction of my first game: "A Space Invader Like"

RetroGaming

Released in 1978, Space Invaders is the first Shoot’em Up. A simple concept: a ship shoots aliens to score points. This title will become legendary alongside the historic Super Mario or the adorable PacMan, and will become a real myth, especially in Japan where this game will become a social phenomenon and will lead to the establishment of more than 350,000 terminals. arcades in the country in 1982. It is therefore in a retro style and inspired by the late 1970s that I choose to create my first arcade-type game. The principle being the same as the initial one, a ship which can move from left to right and which can shoot straight in front of it, must destroy asteroids in order to score points and prevent them from touching the ship or the bottom of it. 'screen. For the creation of this game, we have chosen the Python language. A language as practical as it is simple to use when we are new to the world of coding. Thanks to the Tkinter graphics library, the game was able to have a retro graphics style as we wanted and its realization was most fun and satisfying to play. With the base game finished, a lot of changes and improvements are in the works. The possibility of changing ships, firing styles or obtaining bonuses on certain destroyed asteroids is under development. The game may be released on PC and the code is available on GitHub so that you can play it locally. A possibility of making it playable on a smartphone is being explored!

Posted by Antoine Macheda

What could I have wished for better than to combine passion and work? This project was the way to no longer be a simple player, but to become a creator! Beyond the game, designing software in its entirety and making it interactive in a new language was a most satisfying experience that I will reproduce without hesitation!
